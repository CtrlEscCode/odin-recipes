Vegetable Stir Fry

Vegetable Stir Fry looks scrumptious

This is a colorful and healthy dish of fresh vegetables sauteed in a savory and sweet sauce made with soy sauce, honey, garlic, and ginger. It's loaded with crunchy carrots, broccoli, baby corn, mushrooms, and peppers. This is a versatile and customizable dish that's great for a quick and easy meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon Olive Oil
  • 1 Large Carrot (sliced)
  • 2 cups Medium Broccoli Florets
  • 8 ounces cans Baby Corn Spears (drained)
  • 8 ounces Mushrooms (white or brown, sliced or quartered)
  • 1 Whole Pepper (red, yellow or orange, seeded and sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons Oil (cooking, extra light olive oil or canola)
  • 2 tablespoons Unsalted Butter
  • 3 Garlic Cloves (peeled and minced)
  • 2 teaspoons Ginger (minced)
  • ¼ cup Chicken Broth (or vegetable broth for vegetarian)
  • ½ teaspoon Cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons Low Sodium Soy Sauce (use Tamari for gluten free)
  • 2 tablespoons Honey
  • ¼ teaspoon Hot Sauce (optional, Frank's or Sriracha work great)

Steps

  1. Prep vegetables – Be sure to cut the vegetables in uniform sizes so they cook evenly.
  2. Sautee veggies in a large ski or wok. Add the butter, garlic and ginger.
  3. Make stir fry sauce by whisking together all of the ingredients.
  4. Add the sauce to the vegetables. Simmer stir fry 3 – 4 minutes until sauce thickens, stir as needed.
